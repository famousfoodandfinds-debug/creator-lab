What is Psych Lab?
Psych Lab is a social selling script generator built entirely on buyer psychology. Every hook, body line, pre-close, and CTA is written around the specific desire your product triggers, not a generic template.
Most creators describe their product. Psych Lab describes your buyer. Who they are, what they feel, and what would make them stop scrolling and tap the cart.
The core principle
Sell the person they become, not the product they buy. Every script starts with the buyer's feeling, not the product's features. Features are proof. Feelings convert.
2
Why Buyer Psychology Works
Every purchase decision is driven by a core desire, something the buyer wants to feel, become, or stop experiencing. Psych Lab identifies which desire your product triggers and builds every line of your script around it.
This matters because most scripts describe the product. The scripts that convert describe the buyer. When someone watches your video and thinks "that's me," that's buyer psychology working exactly the way it should.
Pain / Problem Relief
Most urgent driver. People spend to stop hurting.
Emotion / Identity
Who does this product make me?
Dopamine / Instant Gratification
Novelty and quick wins. Low friction impulse.
Urgency / Scarcity
Deadlines override rational thinking.
Status / Upgrade
Signals who they are becoming.
Habit / Routine
Embedded in daily life. Hard to quit.
Trust / Recognition
Familiarity eliminates hesitation.
Convenience
Saves time, removes effort, simplifies life.
How Psych Lab uses this: Every time you generate, the tool reads your product, picks the strongest desire angle, and builds every line of your script to trigger that specific feeling. You don't need to understand the framework to use it, but the more you use it, the sharper your instincts get.
3
How to use the Script Builder
Start by describing your product in the input box. The more specific you are, the better your angles will be. Include the brand name, what it does, and who it's for.
Select price range -- impulse, considered, or premium. This shapes the pacing, energy, and pre-close believability of every script.
Pick your focus angle -- Psych Lab generates 5 distinct buyer desire angles for your product. Each targets a different type of person. Pick the one that matches who you want to speak to in this video.
Choose your mode -- Curated builds one script step by step. Batch generates up to 5 complete scripts at once, each targeting a different angle. Skit generates two-person dialogue scripts with relationship types.
Review your script -- every script includes a hook, body lines, pre-close, CTA, on-screen text options, a ready-to-post caption, and hashtags.
Save and track -- save scripts to your product library, mark videos as posted, and track your daily streak. Your script bank grows with every product you add.
✏️
You can also write your own angle
If you know exactly who you're speaking to, skip the generated angles and type a custom focus. You can add the same custom angle multiple times -- useful in batch and skit mode when you want variations of the same angle targeting different relationship types or buyer moods.
Pro tip: Use batch mode when you need a full day of content fast. Five angles, five complete scripts, one generation. Each targets a completely different buyer so you're not posting the same video five times.
4
Pain vs Aspiration Framing
Before writing your hook, Psych Lab makes a decision most creators never think about. Should this script lead with pain or aspiration? The wrong choice kills the video before the first sentence ends.
Pain Mode
Used when the buyer has a real frustration they feel daily. The hook surfaces that pain and makes it visible. The product becomes the relief. Best for functional products that solve a known problem.
Aspiration Mode
Used when the buyer isn't broken, they're upgrading. The hook paints the life they could be living. The product becomes the doorway into that version of themselves. Best for premium, lifestyle, and gift products.
Both Mode
Lead with the aspiration, the life they want. Close with the quiet cost of not acting. Every day without it is another day in the version they've outgrown. Most powerful for premium home goods and kitchen upgrades.
Psych Lab handles this automatically. It reads your product and buyer angle and chooses the right framing before writing a single word. You don't have to decide, but understanding why it chose what it chose makes you a sharper creator.
5
Setting your Daily Goal
Short-form selling rewards consistency above almost everything else. Set a daily posting goal and Psych Lab tracks your progress with a meter at the top of the screen.
Every day you hit your goal builds your streak. The streak is everything. Creators who post consistently compound their results faster than anyone else, not because they're more talented, but because the algorithm rewards reliability.
The rule
Pick a number you can hit every single day, even on your worst day. A consistent 3 beats an inconsistent 6 every time. Start conservative and build up.

Voice Learning
Psych Lab gets smarter the more you use it. Every time you rate a line with a thumbs up or down, the tool learns which styles match how you actually talk and adjusts every future generation to fit your voice.

👎

·

Good idea, wrong product. Only rate what genuinely reflects your voice.
Important: Only rate lines that genuinely reflect your voice or don't. If a line is just wrong for the product but the style is fine, skip it. Otherwise you're teaching the tool the wrong lesson.
7
Skit Scripts
Skit mode generates two-person dialogue scripts using the same buyer psychology framework as every other mode. The difference is delivery -- instead of one person talking to camera, two people have a conversation the viewer feels like they stumbled into.
Why skits convert
A monologue tells the viewer something. A scene shows them something. When THEM voices the exact skepticism the viewer is feeling and YOU answers it naturally, the viewer stops watching an ad and starts watching themselves.
The four relationship types change everything about the tone, humor, and dynamic:
Friend
Equal energy. Casual sharing, no selling. YOU found something and cannot stop thinking about it. Best for lifestyle, beauty, kitchen, home goods.
Spouse
One skeptic, one believer. Warm domestic humor. The I told you so energy when THEM finally comes around. Works incredibly well for home goods and kitchen.
Stranger
Organic discovery. THEM notices something and asks. YOU is a little surprised but happy to share. Zero selling energy. Feels completely overheard.
Expert
YOU brings specific knowledge THEM does not have. THEM asks the questions the viewer is thinking. Best for supplements, wellness, tech -- anything where the why matters.
Each angle card has its own relationship selector. Tap a relationship, then tap + to add that angle and relationship combination to your batch. You can add the same angle multiple times with different relationships to get variations.
Pro tip: Skit mode generates one script at a time so you see each one appear as it finishes. Five skits takes about 30-40 seconds total -- each one is being written fresh with Sonnet, not templated.
